# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Anyone who wants to track what they eat with minimal friction. Primary user is a non-native English speaker who finds typing inconvenient — voice-first input with real-time captions lets them confirm the transcription before saving. The app serves a personal health goal: identifying foods that may trigger allergic reactions through consistent logging. Eventually a public tool, but designed with one opinionated user in mind first.

Context: mobile, on the go, often right after eating. They want to say a sentence, confirm it, and move on. The interaction should take under 5 seconds for a typical entry.

## Product Purpose

Eliminate the friction of food tracking so it actually happens. Most food diaries fail because they require too much typing, too much discipline, or too much time. Wattefakueat removes all three barriers: voice input for speed, NLP parsing for structure, and local-only storage for privacy. Success means the user logs consistently enough to identify their trigger foods.

## Brand Personality

Calm, clean, premium. Three words that describe the feel:

- **Calm** — the app should feel like a quiet tool, not an urgent dashboard. No alarms, no badges, no pressure.
- **Clean** — uncluttered layouts, generous whitespace, purposeful every element. Nothing decorative.
- **Premium** — feels crafted, not assembled. Attention to micro-detail: timing of transitions, quality of glass, hierarchy of type.

Voice: quiet confidence. Not chatty, not playful, not sterile.

## Anti-references

- No "tech startup" aesthetic. No dark blue, no cyan, no generic SaaS dashboard patterns. The app should not look like it belongs in a Y Combinator batch.
- No childish or gamified elements. No streaks, no achievements, no badges. This is a tool, not a habit coach.
- No over-minimal empty states that leave the user stranded. Empty states should guide, not shrug.

## Design Principles

1. **Voice-first, not voice-only.** The best interaction is saying what you ate and having it work. When voice fails, text is one tap away. Never force dictation.
2. **See before save.** Every transcription is shown to the user before anything is committed. The NLP result is a suggestion, not a verdict. The user always has the last word on what gets logged.
3. **Frictionless consistency.** The entire entry flow should take under 5 seconds for a typical meal. Every extra tap is a future skipped log.
4. **Private by default.** No accounts, no cloud, no data leaving the device. Export is a feature, not an obligation.
5. **Premium restraint.** Use quality materials (glass, depth, light) sparingly. The glass aesthetic is real but reserved for surfaces that benefit from it — cards, overlays, the voice input modal. The rest is clean static layout.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as a baseline. Given voice-first input, captions are essential — both live (during dictation) and static (the final transcription). The interface should work with or without speech recognition available. Colors chosen for sufficient contrast through glass layers.
